# Domain: Advisor Style

These rules capture advisor-derived journal-style compression preferences without changing scientific content.

- Give highest stylistic priority to the shortest wording that states the technical meaning clearly and correctly.
- Do not add explanatory bridge sentences for standard steps unless needed to prevent ambiguity.
- If a sentence is already concise and unambiguous, leave it unchanged rather than polishing for its own sake.
- Prefer subsection titles that directly state the local function or object.
- Use formulas as the center of gravity; prose should define, connect, or constrain them.
- Prefer standard technical lead-ins such as `is defined as`, `can be expressed as`, and `is denoted as`.
- Reduce scaffolding phrases such as `Accordingly`, `For clarity`, and `In the notation adopted here` unless they are necessary.
- Prefer one short sentence per symbol or label when each carries a distinct definitional role.
- Prefer direct definitional verbs such as `distinguishes`, `identifies`, `denotes`, and `represents`.
- State negligible or excluded cases in short standalone sentences after the main taxonomy.
- Keep local definitions ordered by dependency.
- Preserve uncertainty as an explicit question or flagged item rather than silently harmonizing it.
